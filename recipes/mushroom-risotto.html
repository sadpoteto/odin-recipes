Mushroom Risotto

Description

Risotto, a unambiguously italian way to eat rice, by drenching it in broth and cheese. The addition of mushrooms greatly boosts the umami content, making for a decadent and creamy dish that pairs well with most meats (and wine, of course). Great as both a main or side dish at any italian-themed dinner party.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lb sliced portobello mushrooms
  • 1 lb sliced white mushrooms
  • 1.5 cups Arborio rice
  • 0.5 cups dry white wine
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp chopped chives
  • 0.33 cups grated parmesan cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Put the broth in a saucepan, heat on low
  2. Put 2 tbsp of olive oil in a large saucepan, heat on med-high. Add mushrooms and cook until soft, stirring often. Once done, set aside in a bowl.
  3. Add the remaining olive oil to the pan, cook shallots for 1 minute while stirring. Add rice, keep stirring and cook until golden.
  4. Stir constantly while adding wine, keep stirring until it is fully absorbed. Add 0.5 cups of warm broth and repeat.
  5. Keep adding broth in 0.5 cup increments until the rice is tender yet still firm, this should take 15-20 mins.
  6. Remove the pan from the stove, stir in the mushrooms with cheese, butter and chives.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, serve promptly.
